Jeffrey Abbott & Andrew Maynard

This booklet distills the book into six of its sharpest sentences and four of its working models — the ones the AI Salon TLV community will be referencing throughout the evening.

Each model is a small practice you can run in fifteen minutes: a spectrum to locate your work, a matrix to map your identity, a loop to convert defensiveness into curiosity, and a canvas to shape how you talk with AI.

Take notes in the margins. Argue with the quotes. The point of the evening is not to agree — it is to leave with better questions.

03 Why this section

Build consciously.

This section is here to encourage and inspire AI builders to build conscious AI systems.

The quotes and models that follow are not abstractions. They are working tools for people who ship AI products and want to ship better ones — sharper questions, clearer boundaries, a more honest map of what AI can do and what only humans can do.

Read them slowly. Argue with them. Notice which one lands and which one stings. The point is not to agree with the authors — it is to leave the page with sharper questions than you brought to it.

01 Tool · Model

Human Qualities Spectrum

A way to understand which human qualities AI can replicate — and which remain uniquely ours. Not a hierarchy: we need all three.

Replicable Skills AI can master — calculation, pattern, style.
Relational Presence, context, the room — AI joins but misses the current.
Transcendent Meaning, moral imagination — what arises from being human.
Invest yourself

Not a hierarchy — we need all three. But stop pretending the left end makes you irreplaceable.
