02 THE BOOK

A practical guide to thriving with AI.

This booklet distills AI and the Art of Being Human into seven of its sharpest sentences and seven of its working models — the ones the AI Salon TLV community will be referencing throughout the evening.

Each model is a small practice you can run in fifteen minutes: a mirror to see what your system reflects, a spectrum to locate your work, a matrix to map your identity, a loop to convert defensiveness into curiosity, a canvas to shape how you talk with AI, a triangle to conduct AI where the stakes are real, and a starter kit to gather a circle of your own.

Take notes in the margins. Argue with the quotes. The point of the evening is not to agree — it is to leave with better questions.

03 WHY THIS SECTION

Working tools, not abstractions.

The quotes and models that follow are not abstractions. They are working tools for people who ship AI products and want to ship better ones — sharper questions, clearer boundaries, a more honest map of what AI can do and what only humans can do.

Read them slowly. Notice which one lands and which one stings.

ENGAGE — Explore the platform

Work with the models yourself — locate your work on the spectrum, map your identity, run a curiosity loop, scaffold a real prompt. Scan the QR on the back cover.

GO DEEPER — Get the book

The full framework lives in AI and the Art of Being Human by Jeffrey Abbott & Andrew Maynard — available on Amazon. The source for every quote and model here.

01
Start here · The opening diagnostic
01 TOOL · MODEL The Mirror Test

Before you trust what an AI shows you — or ship what it produces — hold it to the mirror. Every algorithm reflects the choices of the people who built and trained it. The question is whether you can see the tint in the glass.

i.
REFLECTION
What is it showing back? Name what the output actually is — your patterns, the data's patterns, a confident surface. The mirror shows what you do, not yet what you should.
ii.
ASSUMPTION
What does it call normal? Ask whose data was loud enough to set the default. A model trained on the loudest voices will present their worldview as neutral. Find the "normal" it quietly assumes.
iii.
THE UN-MIRRORABLE
What can't it show? The context it was never given, the values no one logged, the person the data forgot. What the mirror omits is exactly where your judgment has to live.
RUN IT · 10 MINUTES
Take one real output from a system you're building or using. Ask the three questions in order — reflection, assumption, omission — and write one sentence for each. The omission is usually the most important sentence you'll write today.
"The mirror of AI shows us what we do. The question is: what will we choose to become?"
CHAPTER 3 · P.87

For builders: a system that never asks these questions doesn't become neutral — it becomes a mirror with someone else's values baked in. Conscious building starts by naming the tint.

A way to understand which human qualities AI can replicate — and which remain uniquely ours. Not a hierarchy: we need all three.

REPLICABLE
Skills AI can master — calculation, pattern, style.
RELATIONAL
Presence, context, the room — AI joins but misses the current.
TRANSCENDENT
Meaning, moral imagination — what arises from being human.

INVEST YOURSELF

Not a hierarchy — we need all three. But stop pretending the left end makes you irreplaceable.

AI SALON TLV · READING COMPANION STEP II · INDIVIDUAL → ORGANIZATIONAL
From you, to the room, to the world.

The five models so far pointed inward — they helped you see what AI reflects, locate your work, map your identity, turn defensiveness into curiosity, and shape how you talk with AI. A conscious builder who stops at themselves has done only half the work. The last two models carry the practice outward: first into the organization where you make real decisions, then into a community you convene yourself. This is how a reader becomes a convener.

06
Conduct AI where stakes are real
06 TOOL · MODEL The Orchestration Triangle

Three ways of knowing — Data, Intuition, Context. The point is not to pick one but to conduct all three, so you stay the conductor of AI instead of being conducted by it.

Data (D)
What the model can measure and optimize. Fast, scalable, and blind to everything it wasn't given.
Intuition (I)
The practitioner's felt sense — pattern earned over years that hasn't been written down yet.
Context (C)
The room, the market, the town. What the numbers can't price but everyone will feel.
RUN IT · 15 MINUTES
Write tomorrow's biggest decision in the center of a triangle. Mark where it's currently landing — and name the corner going silent. If you're leaning entirely on the model's data, say so out loud. Then ask what changes when intuition and context are given a vote again.
"The real returns come from integration. Not balance — integration."
"Locally intelligent, not just artificially intelligent."
CHAPTER 8 · P.204–206

07
Gather others — start a circle of your own
07 TOOL · MODEL The Micro-Circle Starter Kit

The smallest viable community is three people and a check-in. You don't need thirty, a venue, or permission — just two others willing to show up, and a way to open the room.

If the room wants to keep meeting, work through the Starter Charter on the right.

OPEN THE CIRCLE · THE THREE-QUESTION CHECK-IN

CURIOSITY
What made you curious this week — one thing AI did that you didn't expect?
CONCERN
What raised a concern — where did something feel off, unfair, or too easy?
CARE
Where did care lead you — what did you choose to protect or slow down for?

THE STARTER CHARTER · FOUR QUESTIONS, 30 MINUTES

i.
PURPOSE
One sentence: why this circle exists.
ii.
NORMS
How you'll be together — candor, confidentiality, no selling.
iii.
CADENCE
Together, how often, how long, where. Small and sustainable wins.
iv.
ROLES
Who convenes each time. Rotate, so no hierarchy hardens.

Start or find a circle in your city. AI Salon is a global community of AI enthusiasts across 70+ cities — and always room for one more. aisalon.ai
